# A MILLION LITTLE FIBERS

By

Toweleeeie

## Chapter One

All my life I've been a pretty irresponsible towel.
I thought I was somehow stronger and more immune than other towels.
But as I pursued my derelict lifestyle of getting high and forgetting things, I realized something important. I'm not sure what it was, but I'm pretty sure it was important. Maybe it was about Doritos. No wait, I think it was about how I was created in a lab. Yeah, that's it.

I was made by these scientists at TST, which stands for Towelie Safety Testing or something. They put a computer chip in me that could determine how dry someone was. Pretty high-tech stuff for a towel, right? I don't really remember much about the lab days. Mostly I just remember wanting to get high.

"Don't forget to bring a towel!" That was supposed to be my main purpose, but honestly, I think they just wanted me to be a gimmick. Like those singing fish on plaques, but less annoying. Actually, about the same amount of annoying.

## Chapter Two

Hey, remember that time I met those kids in South Park? Stan, Kyle, Cartman, and that other one who always dies? They were pretty cool, I guess. Well, except Cartman. He was kind of a dick. But I helped them out with something. I think it had to do with a video game or something. Or maybe it was about some aliens? Man, I really should stop getting high so much.

What I do remember is that they needed a towel, and I was there. That's what matters, right? Being there when people need you? Even if you're high and don't really know what's going on?

Actually, I think I might have caused more problems than I solved. But that's just how it goes sometimes when you're a genetically engineered towel with a substance abuse problem.

## Chapter Three

So there was this one time I was living with these guys who made me do all this stuff. I think they were with the government or something. They would test me and have me dry things and remind people to bring towels. It was pretty boring work, not gonna lie.

The worst part was they wouldn't let me get high. Can you imagine? A towel who can't get high? That's like... like a towel that doesn't dry things. It just doesn't make sense.

I remember I escaped from that place. Or did I get fired? Hmm. Either way, I ended up on my own, which was both scary and liberating. Mostly scary because I didn't have anyone to get high with. But mostly liberating because I could get high whenever I wanted.

## Chapter Four

Have you ever tried to get a job as a towel? It's not easy, let me tell you. People look at you funny when you walk into an interview. "We're looking for someone with experience," they say. "In what?" I ask. "Not in being a towel," they say.

I tried working at this Chinese restaurant once. P.F. Chang's. Man, that was a disaster. All I had to do was mix the Chang sauce and take orders, but I kept getting confused. "How spicy would you like your Chang sauce?" I'd ask, and then I'd forget what they said. Or I'd just keep mixing the sauce for like fifteen minutes.

The head waiter fired me. "You're a towel!" he said. "You're a towel!" I replied, because that's my go-to comeback. It didn't help my case.

## Chapter Five

You know what's really hard when you're a towel? Paying rent. I mean, where do towels even get money? I tried doing odd jobs, but nobody wants to hire a towel. I tried panhandling, but people would just look at me and say "You're a towel!" and I'd say "You're a towel!" back, and then we'd just be at an impasse.

One time I tried to sell some of my stuff to make rent. But what stuff does a towel have? Other towels? That seems kind of weird, like selling your own family. And I don't have a family. That I know of. Maybe I do, somewhere in that lab. Cousin Washcloth? Uncle Handtowel? Who knows?

## Chapter Six

So I had this idea once to write a book about my life. I figured people love memoirs, right? And my life has been pretty interesting, what with the being created in a lab and the escaping and the hanging out with those South Park kids and all.

I wrote it all down on this old typewriter I found. It was hard because towels don't really have fingers, so I had to kind of flop down on the keys. It took a long time, and a lot of the pages just had random letters on them. But I thought it was pretty good.

I took it to this publisher, and he was like "Nobody wants to read a towel's autobiography." Can you believe that? I was crushed. I mean, sure, chapters 4 through 8 were mostly about Doritos, but still. It had heart.

## Chapter Seven

After the publisher rejected my book, I was pretty down. I needed money for rent, and I'd just wasted three weeks writing my memoirs. I decided I needed to shape up, focus, and come up with real solutions to my problems.

So naturally, I got high.

And you know what? It worked! I had this brilliant idea. What if I just... changed some things in my book? Made it seem like it was written by a person instead of a towel? I'd just cross out "towel" and write "person" and cross out "towels" and write "people." Simple!

I even got a fake mustache and a little fedora to wear to my meetings with publishers. Disguise complete!

## Chapter Eight

The new publisher loved the book! He was all like "Oh my God, I can't believe a human being has led this kind of life!" and "This is so heartbreaking!" I think the part that really got him was when I wrote "I am a person who often gets hung out to dry by all those around me." Get it? Because towels get hung out to dry? But he thought I was being metaphorical!

I told him my name was Steven McTowelie. He didn't even question it. People really don't pay attention to details, you know? If I had said my name was Steven McHamburger, he probably would have been like "Oh, that's a nice name."

## Chapter Nine

So then I went on Oprah. OPRAH! Can you believe it? Little old me, a towel, on Oprah! I had to wear my disguise, of course. The mustache and hat.

Oprah said my book was "so honest and moving" that it changed her life. She made it her Book of the Month selection! I was gonna be rich! No more worrying about rent!

But something weird happened during the interview. Oprah's crotch kept talking. Like, in a British accent. It was saying stuff like "Nobody knows what it's like to be Oprah's minge." I thought I was just really high, but apparently that was a real thing that was happening. Oprah's genitals were alive and talking. And not happy about being ignored.

## Chapter Ten

After the Oprah appearance, my book sold like crazy! There were posters of me on buses, I was doing book signings, and money was just rolling in. It was great! I even bought this really nice bong. I mean, for decorative purposes. Mostly.

But then things started to go wrong. Geraldo Rivera, of all people, did this "investigative report" where he revealed that I was actually a towel. How did he find out? I later learned it was Oprah's talking minge and asshole who tipped him off. They were trying to get Oprah in trouble so she'd have to quit working and pay attention to them.

Can you believe that? A minge and an asshole conspired against me. That's got to be the weirdest sentence I've ever said. And I've said a lot of weird things when I'm high.

## Chapter Eleven

So after Geraldo exposed me on Larry King Live, things went downhill fast. People were protesting outside my hotel, calling me a liar and demanding their money back. These Latino protesters were particularly upset because apparently I called Geraldo a "beaner towel" while I was high on live TV. I don't really remember that, but it sounds like something I might do when I'm high.

Oprah called me and said we needed to do damage control. She wanted me to come back on her show and explain that I had "heightened" certain things in my book to make it more relevant to people. She made it sound like it was no big deal, like everyone would understand.

I should have known better than to trust a woman whose genitals talk to her.

## Chapter Twelve

When I went back on Oprah, she totally turned on me! She called me a "LYING SACK OF SHIT" and got the audience all riled up. Then she told them to look under their seats, and they all had torches! Actual torches! Like they were going to do a old-fashioned lynching!

I had to run for my life! Through the streets of Chicago, with Oprah and her torch-wielding mob chasing me! It was like a nightmare, except I was awake. Or was I? Sometimes when I'm high it's hard to tell.

I ended up at this bank, but I couldn't get in because the doors were too heavy for a towel to open. I was trapped!

## Chapter Thirteen

So then things got really weird. Like, really, really weird. Oprah's pants ripped, and a gun popped out of her crotch. Her minge had taken control of her body! It was waving the gun around, demanding a helicopter and a jet to take them to France.

And then her asshole started talking too, in a different British accent. They were arguing with each other while holding everyone hostage. The minge shot a police officer! It was chaos!

Everyone was looking at me like I should do something. "You got us into this, towel," they said. "Think of something!" But how am I supposed to think of something when I'm not high? That's when I get my best ideas!

## Chapter Fourteen

I really, really wanted to get high. Every fiber of my being (get it? Fiber? Because I'm a towel?) was screaming for me to light up. But I knew that every time I got high, I came up with ideas that got me in more trouble.

"You need to come up with ideas of what to say on Oprah," my joint whispered to me. Wait, did my joint just talk to me? Or was I imagining it? Or was this like Oprah's minge situation, where inanimate objects just sometimes talk?

I decided to stay strong. No getting high. I was going to solve this problem with a clear head. Which is really hard when you're a towel who's been high pretty much constantly for your entire existence.

## Chapter Fifteen

So I did it. I saved the day. While Oprah's minge was distracted, I managed to slide under the bank doors and let all the hostages escape. Then the SWAT team shot Oprah's asshole (which I felt kind of bad about, even though he was kind of a jerk), and her minge got all dramatic and shot herself.

Oprah survived, but her genitals didn't make it. The police lieutenant said I had a "great idea" to save the hostages. The same people who had been trying to kill me earlier were now apologizing.

I learned an important lesson that day. I shouldn't get high to come up with ideas. I should come up with ideas and then get high, to reward myself.

So that's exactly what I did.

*The End*

...or is it? I don't really remember. I'm pretty high right now.
